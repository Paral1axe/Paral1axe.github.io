Peugeot 205 Turbo 16

A Hot Hatch Rally Monster

Built to compete in the wild era of Group B, the racing T16 dominated the series in the mid-Eighties.

With its 197bhp sent to all four wheels by a turbocharged, fuel-injected 1.8-litre four-pot with a 16-valve cylinder head, it is no wonder how competing with this monster was a challenge.

Here's the story of this french engineering marvel:

  • 1983 - The 205 is introduced as a replacement of the 104 and is homologued.
  • 1984 WRC - Wins the 34th 1000 Lakes Rally, the 26th Rallye Sanremo and the 33rd Lombard RAC Rally.
  • 1984 - Wins What Car?'s Car of the Year award.
  • 1985 WRC - Wins the 53rd Rallye Automobile de Monte Carlo, the 35th International Swedish Raaly, the 19th Rallye de Portugal Vinho do Porto, the 32nd Acropolis Rally, the 15th AWA Clarion Rally of New Zealand, the 5th Marlboro Rally Argentina and the 35th 1000 Lakes Rally.
  • 1986 WRC - Wins the 36th International Swedish Rally, the 30th Tour de Corse - Rallye de France, the 33rd Acropolis Rally, the 36th 1000 Lakes Rally and the 35th Lombard RAC Rally.
  • 1990 - Declared "car of the decase" by CAR Magazine.
  • 1992 - Sets the quarter-mile, half-kilometer and kilometer world records in Category A3 Group 3.
